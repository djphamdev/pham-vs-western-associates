Key Allegations

  • National origin discrimination in violation of California FEHA (Gov. Code §12940)
  • Disparate treatment in compensation, workload, and work schedule
  • Disparate scrutiny of timekeeping and attendance
  • Comparator evidence: Japanese-national employee treated more favorably
  • Constructive discharge following complaints
  • Failure to investigate or remediate after internal complaints

Why This Case May Be Legally Significant

  • Comparator evidence — direct side-by-side with same-role employee of different national origin
  • Documented pattern — 200+ pieces of evidence across 4 years
  • Parent company involvement — Japanese parent company (WAI) directives affecting U.S. subsidiary
  • CRD pattern — prior similar complaints at related entities
  • Damages documentation — wage records, profit data, performance metrics
